About Jasem Shuman

Jasem Shuman, Palestinian Artist & Sculptor

Contemporary Palestinian Artist & Sculptor

MA Contemporary Arts, Educator & Curator

Jasem J M Mousa (Jasem Shuman) is an internationally recognized Palestinian artist with over 15 years of professional practice. His multidisciplinary work spans sculpture, painting, film, and community education, serving as both autobiography and resistance through art.

Born and raised in Palestine, Jasem's artistic journey began during the Second Intifada, when art became his safe space for expression after being forced to work as a construction worker when war prevented his university education. Since 2006, he has dedicated himself to creating art that tells Palestinian stories and serves his community.

Artist Statement

"Every Palestinian must tell his story."

My pieces are autobiographical and serve as visual translations of my emotional states toward various themes: life, self, society, love, language, dreams, and their role in relationships and communication. Artistic Practice & Philosophy

Jasem works across diverse mediums including painting and coloring, sculpture on clay and stone, granite works in large urban scales, metalworking with iron and industrial materials, and installation works. He also engages in poetry and film direction when opportunities arise, viewing art as a multifaceted tool for storytelling and community healing.

Urban sculptural works represent his greatest passion, as he sees in them tremendous ability to reach society with thought, art, and beauty, and to improve the environment around us. Through these public interventions, he can tell his story to the largest possible number of people and add visual testimonies to the civilization surrounding us.

Having become one of the few specialists in psychological relief through arts in his community, Jasem's work extends beyond personal expression to serve as a healing mechanism for collective trauma and a means of cultural preservation and resistance.
